Pit Stop

Best Practices for AI at Projection

The 4 Principles

  • Always verify AI output
  • Protect sensitive data
  • AI assists, YOU decide
  • Ask for sources

Principle 1: Always Verify

AI makes mistakes. Confidently.

It once told Jose a breakout was at 9am. It was 10:30.

Trust but verify — EVERY time.

Principle 2: Protect Sensitive Data

External AI (ChatGPT, etc.)

  • Don't paste screenshots of IE, CRM, financials
  • Use fake names for clients (Hubba Bubba!)
  • Be thoughtful about what you share

Internal Projection AI (coming soon)

  • Built for confidential use
  • Safe for real data

Principle 3: AI Assists, You Decide

AI is your co-pilot, not your autopilot.

  • You're responsible for the final output
  • Your name is on that email
  • Your expertise validates the answer

Principle 4: Ask for Sources

"Where did you get that information?"

  • AI can cite sources and provide links
  • Makes verification easier
  • Helps you learn, not just copy

The 3 Don'ts

  • Don't screenshot confidential documents
  • Don't trust blindly
  • Don't skip the human touch

Don't Screenshot Confidential Docs

Never paste these into external AI

  • IntelliEvent screens
  • Sugar CRM data
  • Client financials
  • Credit card authorizations
  • Contracts

Don't Trust Blindly

AI will never say "I don't know"

It will make something up instead.

Verify numbers. Verify dates. Verify names.
Verify anything that matters.

Don't Skip the Human Touch

Clients know when something is AI-generated.

It sounds… off. Robotic. Generic.

  • Add your personality
  • Rewrite in your voice
  • Make it human

Important Clarifications

  • These are BEST PRACTICES, not Projection policy
  • AI usage is ENCOURAGED but not required
  • Same rules for built-in AI (Copilot, Adobe AI, etc.)
  • Use your judgment — you know your clients
